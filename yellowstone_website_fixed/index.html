About Yellowstone

Yellowstone National Park was the first national park in the world, established in 1872. It covers over 2 million acres across Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho. The park is famous for geysers, hot springs, waterfalls, and canyons. Wildlife is abundant: you can see bison, elk, bears, wolves, and eagles. Yellowstone’s landscapes range from mountain peaks and forests to rivers and geothermal features. Visiting Yellowstone gives you a chance to connect with nature and experience America’s original wilderness. Fun fact: Old Faithful erupts approximately every 90 minutes.

Things To Do

  1. Watch Geysers & Hot Springs – See Old Faithful erupt and explore geothermal areas.
  2. Hiking & Nature Walks – Trails range from easy boardwalks to challenging mountain hikes.
  3. Wildlife Viewing – Look for bison, elk, bears, and wolves safely.
  4. Camping – Campgrounds inside the park; book early for sunrise/sunset experience.
  5. Photography & Scenic Drives – Drive along Yellowstone Lake, Lamar Valley, and Hayden Valley.
  6. Ranger Programs – Attend talks or guided walks to learn about wildlife and geology.

Things To Avoid

  1. Never Feed Wildlife – Dangerous for you and them.
  2. Stay on Marked Trails – Protect fragile areas and yourself.
  3. Avoid Approaching Bears or Wolves – Keep safe distance.
  4. Do Not Disturb Natural Features – Leave geysers and rivers intact.
  5. Follow Park Rules & Signs – Safety and conservation first.
  6. Don’t Litter – Take all trash with you.
